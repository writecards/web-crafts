The cyberfeminist manifesto “articulates feminism on the internet as visceral—related to *viscera,* slime, wetware, and birth. It still feels that way: messy, howling, but very much alive.” this reminds me of glitch feminism. Notably, the glitch is ephemeral and exists only in the present. I like to think of this akin encouraging marginalized identities to be unashamed to take up space in a cis-het-patriarchal society. VNS matrix produced the cyberfeminist manifesto in 1992, which feels way ahead of its time. It is a spectacular creation that I’m glad was produced. Glitch studies manifesto reminded me of the world’s first glitch-art video, titled digital tv dinner. It was created by Jamie Faye Fenton, a trans woman who played a significant role in early days of adobe. The video was made by her smashing video game cartridges repeatedly while removing them from the console - a supercut of hundreds of hours of her labor. watch it here [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ad9zdlaRvdM&ab_channel=JamieFayeFenton](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ad9zdlaRvdM&ab_channel=JamieFayeFenton)
