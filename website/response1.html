shengli wen

I really appreciate Laurel Schwulst’s thoughts on internet/social media/and the personal webpage in a contemporary context. I see a lot of my own feelings echoed in her words. For one, Schwulst mentions the “technological frictions and social pressures” that come with creating and maintaining a website in 2018, which is unfortunately so true in an era where social media’s goal from the corporate perspective is ultimately to make profit off of our attention and bodies. I also find her statement on the website’s potential to be a self-reflexive loop to be quite interesting - “when you put energy into a website, in turn the website helps form your own identity.” As an ‘artist’ who is wrapping up undergrad, I resonate with this on a personal level as I’ve struggled with the “About Me” section on my portfolio - as the thought of defining myself in a few sentences has always been rather anxiety inducing. I’ve recently shifted my perspective to treat myself and my website with more kindness, accepting that whatever I write now can be updated later (similar to her ‘website as garden’ metaphor). A mutual friend named Tiger Dingsun is a web developer and graphic designer who works with text. His undergrad thesis, which I find relevant to this week’s theme, was a series of 18 websites that demonstrate various ways of re-imagining what reading/presenting text can look like and examining the webpage as a form of time-based media. He actually interviewed Laurel Schwulst during his design process, on her thoughts generating text (what a coincidence!). His (amazing) thesis is here: [**http://tdingsun.github.io/dp/**](http://tdingsun.github.io/dp/)
