Website for Donna Haraway's "A Cyborg Manifesto" - shengli wen

Background

Donna Haraway's "A Cyborg Manifesto" was published in 1985. It is about rejecting regid boundaries between human/animal/machine. The manifesto criticizes traditional gender and feminist norms, and it's cited as a major influence in the femist posthuman circles.

Process

Donna Haraway's work on ecology has been incredibly inspiring. I wanted to take this assignment as an opportunity to dive into her Cyborg Manifesto. I must admit, it was a really difficult and dense text for me to process. This influenced how I decided to present the text on the screen.

I started by creating a home page with five sections, mirroring the subsections in the manifsto. I wanted to create an abstract layout for the home page, with slow moving css animations to create an ambient effect.

Since the manifesto is over 60 pages, in the time frame of this class, I've only finished coding the first three sections. The first section's content is presented in a large textsize with gradient shadow. The user can click through the text one sentence at a time. This was intended to help viewers digest the text slower.

The second section is a series of squares with an image of California's San Andreas Faltline in the background. This illudes to a quote I read in the cyberfeminism index, which says the manifesto had an earthquake effect and jolted academia and cyberculture in general. The text scrolls super fast here, mimicing the speed and intensity of the influence of this text.

The third section all on one page, with alternating blurriness on each sentence. This again was intended to create visual emphasis on each sentence as the viewer browses through.

Reflection

What started as a daunting task morphed into an exploration on the possibilities of CSS. I hope to finish the rest of the pages over winter break, and fix some of the readability and functionality of the current pages. I showed this site to my roommate, who said: "I love this but find it a little disturbing." I want to lean into this feeling more.
