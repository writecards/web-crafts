In the introduction, Galloway notes that with a stroke of a delete key, a whole country could be swept off the internet (9). This made me curious about how internet censorship works in mainland China. According to wikipedia (take this with a grain of salt), the government blocks website content and internet access - and major internet platforms in China established elaborate self-censorship mechanisms. “Methods used to block websites and pages include [DNS spoofing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNS_spoofing), blocking access to [IP addresses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IP_address), analyzing and filtering [URLs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/URL), packet inspection, and resetting connections.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_censorship_in_China#cite_note-howto-3)” here’s a simplified topology of the chinese firewall [https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Topology_of_the_Chinese_firewall.svg/1200px-Topology_of_the_Chinese_firewall.svg.png](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Topology_of_the_Chinese_firewall.svg/1200px-Topology_of_the_Chinese_firewall.svg.png) ![Untitled](https://s3-us-west-2.amazonaws.com/secure.notion-static.com/5fec097e-8fe6-42a6-a978-422b00459b1f/Untitled.png) What’s interesting to me is the dynamic aspect of China’s censorship policies. Earlier this year when Russia first invaded Ukraine, to combat disinformation, several Chinese social platforms announced that they will display user locations based on IP addresses, which display the user’s province or region. Google was banned in China in 2002. A mirror website elgooG was created by All Too Flat, which was initially made for fun but had practical use for people in China. I grew up as an expat in Lanzhou China. I remember my family having a VPN where we could pick different locations to access the internet from (perhaps we were actually changing our IP address?). My memory of this is hazy but I do recall youtube being censored off and on. Chinese internet users’ evasion of internet censorship is also ridiculously amazing. A common way is to use analogies to discuss topics. Evasion is a form of internet activism.
